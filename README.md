## So Which Plan Actually Makes Sense For You?

Let's be real — "unlimited" is one of those words that gets thrown around so much in hosting that it barely means anything anymore. HostBas does the same thing. The real differentiators are the **resource limits** buried in the tech specs table: CPU allocation, RAM, entry processes, and inode count. Those numbers tell you a lot more than "unlimited SSD" ever will.

### Hero — The Long-Game Budget Pick

The Hero plan is the one that makes you stop and look twice. **€2.19 per 3 years** sounds like a typo. It's not — it's the renewal rate after the initial €79 promotional payment. That's genuinely one of the lowest long-term hosting costs you'll find anywhere right now.

The catch? You're working with 50% CPU share, 512 MB RAM, and only 5 entry processes at a time. That's fine for a personal blog, a small portfolio, or a landing page. It's not fine if you expect any real traffic spikes or want to run resource-hungry plugins.

No Imunify360, no SSH access, no PHP Selector. It's barebones by design — aimed squarely at beginners who just need something online.

👉 [Start with the Hero plan](https://hostbas.com/user/index.php?rp=/store/shared-hosting/heroo&billingcycle=triennially&aff=99)

### Advanced — The Sweet Spot

The Advanced plan is where things get genuinely interesting. For €9.99/year (after the initial €79), you get Imunify360 security, SSH access, PHP Selector, MultiPHP Manager, Git version control, and a bump to 75% CPU and 768 MB RAM.

That's not "advanced" in name only — those features actually matter. If you're running a client site, a small business website, or a WordPress blog you're serious about, this is where you'd want to be. The 10 entry processes (vs. 5 on Hero) also means you can handle light-to-medium traffic without everything grinding to a halt.

At under €10/year on renewal, it's a pretty compelling offer for what you get.

### Elite — When You Actually Need Power

The Elite plan isn't for everyone, but if you've ever hit resource limits on shared hosting before, you'll appreciate what's here: 200% CPU, 2 GB RAM, 30 entry processes, and 1,024 IOPS. That's a meaningful jump.

You also unlock Node.js, Python, Ruby, terminal access, ClamAV, advanced email tools, and the ability to import directly from cPanel or DirectAdmin. It's the plan for developers, agencies managing multiple client sites, or anyone running a moderately busy WooCommerce store.

At €14.99/month, it's more expensive than the other tiers — but then again, those other tiers aren't trying to do what Elite does.

## What's Powering the Servers?

All three plans run on **LiteSpeed Web Server** with **LSCache support**, which is a good sign. LiteSpeed consistently outperforms Apache on WordPress sites, and LSCache is one of the better caching solutions available. Add CloudLinux LVE (which isolates each account so one spammy neighbor can't tank your site), and the infrastructure story is reasonably solid.

The control panel is cPanel, which has been the industry standard forever. Whether you love or hate cPanel, the fact is that everyone already knows how to use it.

**99.9% uptime** is the stated guarantee. Users on Trustpilot have generally backed this up — one noted that setup was easy and "uptime has been solid" with speed better than their previous host. Another user, an IT consultant, called the overall experience professional, noting that support responded within minutes even late at night.

## The LiteSpeed + cPanel Stack Is Worth Mentioning

A lot of budget hosts cut corners on the server stack. They'll give you "unlimited storage" while running ancient Apache configurations on overcrowded servers. HostBas uses LiteSpeed across all plans, which does make a real difference for page load times, especially on WordPress.
